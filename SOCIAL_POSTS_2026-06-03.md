# Social posts — 2026-06-03

## LinkedIn

Dutch student housing is not only about rent headlines.

Kences data reported via NOS (September 2025) shows a quieter shift: fewer students are even searching for a room after years of shortage. Meanwhile, 57% of graduates still live in their student room one year after finishing, and net room supply in twenty student cities fell by an estimated 13,500 units.

The gap between who lives out (44%) and who wants to (49%) is widening compared with eight years ago, when those shares were 52% and 59%.

For universities, municipalities, and student wellbeing teams, the lesson is pipeline thinking: search behaviour, graduate turnover, and landlord exits are linked. Treating housing only as a first-year intake problem misses where rooms actually get stuck.

Read the full evidence summary: https://domumatch.com/blog/student-room-search-giving-up-netherlands

Sources: NOS reporting on Kences Landelijke Monitor Studentenhuisvesting (3 September 2025).

## Instagram

Be honest: have you ever stopped refreshing room listings because it felt pointless?

You are not alone. Dutch monitoring data shows fewer students even search for a room now, while many graduates cannot move out of student rooms because the regular market is locked.

That combo means:
- Longer commutes if you stay at home
- Fewer rooms freeing up for the next cohort
- More stress before the semester even starts

If you did find a place: talk about sleep, guests, and money before small frictions become big ones. In a tight market, a mid-year move is expensive in time you do not get back.

What is harder right now for you: finding a listing, affording rent, or making a match work once you are in?
