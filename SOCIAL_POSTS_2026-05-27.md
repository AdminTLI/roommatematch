# Social posts - 2026-05-27

## LinkedIn

Dutch student housing is often discussed as a rent-and-supply spreadsheet. New CBS and NIDI figures, reported by NOS in February 2026, add a mobility lens: among 2023 graduates, 43 percent had lived with parents for the full five years before graduation, compared with 31 percent for the 2016 cohort, and those who did move out did so later in the programme. That is address data, not a mood survey, yet it constrains how much evening campus life is physically feasible without long commutes. Layer that beside Kences monitor summaries (wish versus reality for rented rooms) and Nuffic’s 2023 finding that 37 percent of international leavers cited unsuitable housing as a key reason, and the policy file stops looking like a side annex to enrolment. Municipal pages in Breda and Tilburg show the other half of the story: lawful room supply is routed through permits, planning checks, and institution mailboxes, which shapes where pressure lands in real cities.

## Instagram

The numbers behind “I still live at home” changed faster than the campus memes. Recent Dutch reporting puts sharper percentages on who never moved out during their whole degree, and how that split differs between HBO and university tracks. Housing is not only a price tag; it is also distance to friends, labs, and quiet study space after a late train. If you have lived both worlds, what actually shifted your routine more: rent, commute, or not finding anything at all?
