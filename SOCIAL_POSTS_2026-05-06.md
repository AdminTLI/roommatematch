## LinkedIn (universities / municipalities / retention leads)

Student housing is rarely measured as academic infrastructure—until retention dashboards move.

Recent Dutch reporting ties the squeeze directly to student pathways: more graduates living at home through their studies, international enrolments softening, and persistent friction for mobile students searching for rooms (DutchNews, 2025–2026). Peer-reviewed work links inadequate housing to poorer mental health—and mental health to academic performance (Imran et al., 2025, *Frontiers in Education*).

The ROI is upstream: predictable intake timelines, scam-aware guidance, transparent shared norms, and behavioural roommate matching reduce mid-year moves and invisible cognitive load.
