# Social posts - 20 May 2026

Editorial: International student housing in the Netherlands (data and integration risk).

## LinkedIn

Dutch student housing is no longer only a campus services story. Public reporting on Kences figures shows a widening gap between students who want a rented room and students who have one, with national shortage estimates now discussed in the tens of thousands of rooms. Nuffic survey work adds a retention angle: among international graduates who left the Netherlands, 37 percent cited not finding proper housing as an important reason, alongside the same share flagging financing. When municipalities route private landlords to university mailboxes, as Breda does for Avans and BUas under the hospitaregeling, you see how tightly housing search, institutions, and integration outcomes are coupled. Worth reading if you work on internationalisation, wellbeing, or city policy.

## Instagram

Some of you are refreshing Kamernet at 2 a.m. while also trying to pass exams. The numbers back what that feels like: fewer students now live in a room than say they want one, and surveys show housing sits next to money as a reason people leave after graduating. You are not dramatic for saying the search affects your headspace. What helped you stay grounded while hunting for a room: a housemate group chat, a study buddy, or something else?
